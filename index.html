Research Experience

Quantum phase transitions based on deep learning

2022–2023

Advisor: Dr. Ming Xue

Content: Applied deep neural networks to determine phase boundaries in quantum many-body systems (Ising-Hubbard, spin-1 Heisenberg, and spin-1 BBQ). Implemented an unsupervised Differential Cooperation Network (DCN) architecture combining convolutional and competitive layers to classify two-dimensional ground-state phase diagrams.

Contribution: Built the full DCN pipeline in TensorFlow, performed data preprocessing from Monte Carlo simulations, and analyzed feature-map clustering without predefined labels. Identified compatibility issues between excitation spectra and network topology, and proposed dimensional input dimensionality to achieve full-phase map convergence.

Black hole volume and its correction under higher-order derivative gravity

2023–2024

Advisor: Dr. Yuxin An

Content: Investigated the Christodoulou-Rovelli (C-R) volume, which quantifies black hole interior spacelike hypersurface area, under higher-order Riemann gravity in AdS spacetime. Studied how curvature topology (k = 1, 0, −1) and coupling constants modify the time linear growth and thermodynamic interpretation.

Contribution: Derived the C-R volume analytically from the metric function, performed symbolic integration in Mathematica, and verified the persistence of linear growth via numerical evolution. Clarified the relation between higher-curvature corrections and the complexity–volume conjecture in holographic gravity.

Covariant Phase Space Approach to ADM conserved quantities and First Law of Black Hole Thermodynamics

2025–2026

Advisor: Dr. Stefan Hollands

Content: Started from the ADM formalism of General Relativity and obtained ADM conserved quantities from the surface term of the ADM action. Developed a covariant phase space formulation of ADM charges and the first law of black hole thermodynamics for general diffeomorphism-invariant and higher-curvature gravity theories, where ADM charges and black-hole entropy arise as Noether charges at spatial infinity and on the horizon.

Contribution: Derived Lee–Wald symplectic potential, Noether charge, and ADM conserved quantities in the covariant formalism; demonstrated the first law from charge variations; extended the analysis to f(R) gravity to compute corrections to conserved quantities and Wald entropy; and computed the ADM mass and angular momentum at spatial infinity for Schwarzschild and Kerr geometries.
